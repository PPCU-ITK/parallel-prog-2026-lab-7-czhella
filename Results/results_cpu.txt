In case of lower resolution grids (200 x 100), CPU and GPU performances are similar since there is less room
for data-level parallelism and GPU overhead is quite prominent in such cases.
However, with increasing grid size, the GPU becomes significantly more efficient by exploiting data parallelism.
At the largest resolution (3200×1600), the GPU achieves approximately a 24× speedup compared to the CPU.
Overall, the results highlight that GPU acceleration is most effective for larger,
compute-intensive problems while maintaining numerical accuracy.
